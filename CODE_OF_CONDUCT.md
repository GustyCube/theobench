# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want TheoBench to be a respectful, technically serious project.

Participants in this project are expected to make discussion, review, and collaboration constructive for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful in technical disagreement.
- Critique ideas, code, data, and process directly.
- Assume good intent, but do not excuse harmful behavior.
- Keep benchmark discussion grounded in evidence.
- Respect maintainer decisions on scope, release timing, and moderation.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Publishing private information without permission
- Repeated bad-faith arguments after moderation
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional engineering setting

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that violate these standards.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its maintainers.

## Enforcement

Report unacceptable behavior through the repository's issue tracker or by contacting the maintainers directly through the repository account.

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including warning, temporary suspension, or permanent ban from project spaces.
